Global AI Policy Timeline
2019–2025
2019
OECD AI Principles adopted
First intergovernmental standard on AI, endorsed by 46 countries. Establishes voluntary principles on transparency, accountability, and human-centred AI.
2021
EU AI Act proposed by European Commission
First comprehensive horizontal AI legislation globally. Introduces risk-based classification of AI systems.
2021
China releases ethical norms for new generation AI
Ministry of Science and Technology publishes governance principles with state-directed focus.
2022
China Algorithm Recommendation Regulations effective
World's first binding regulation on algorithmic recommendation systems. Sets global precedent.
2022
US NIST AI Risk Management Framework published
Voluntary framework for AI risk management adopted by federal agencies and industry.
2023
China Generative AI Regulations enter force
World's first law specifically governing generative AI services. Requires security assessments and content compliance.
2023
US Executive Order 14110 on AI signed
Most significant US federal AI action. Directs agencies on AI safety, security, and governance requirements.
2023
UK Bletchley Declaration on AI safety signed
28 countries including US, EU, and China sign first multilateral AI safety declaration at Bletchley Park summit.
2024
EU AI Act officially published in EU Official Journal
Enters force August 2024. First prohibitions apply. Full application of high-risk rules by August 2026.
2025
UPCOMING: GPAI model obligations apply (EU AI Act)
General Purpose AI model governance requirements effective August 2025. Major compliance milestone.
2026
PROJECTED: Full EU AI Act high-risk application
All high-risk AI system requirements become applicable. Conformity assessments required.
